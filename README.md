**Air Quality Analysis Dashboard | Power BI Project**

Air pollution has become one of the most pressing environmental and public health challenges in India. Rapid urbanization, industrial growth, increased vehicle usage, construction activities, and seasonal factors have significantly contributed to the deterioration of air quality across many cities. Monitoring and understanding air pollution patterns is essential for policymakers, researchers, and citizens to take informed action.

To explore these patterns and uncover meaningful insights, I developed an "Air Quality Analysis Dashboard using Power BI". This interactive dashboard analyzes Air Quality Index (AQI) data across different states and cities in India, helping users understand pollution trends, identify hotspots, and analyze pollutant contributions.

📊 Project Overview

The **Air Quality Analysis Dashboard** provides a comprehensive and interactive view of air pollution levels across India. The main goal of the project is to transform raw environmental data into meaningful insights that can support better decision-making.

Air quality data can often be complex and difficult to interpret due to the large number of monitoring stations, pollutants, and time-based measurements involved. This dashboard simplifies the process by presenting the data in a clear, visual, and user-friendly format.

Users can explore air quality data from multiple perspectives, including:

* **State-level air pollution patterns**
* **City-level AQI comparisons**
* **Pollutant contribution analysis**
* **Monitoring station performance**
* **Geographic distribution of pollution hotspots**

The dashboard uses interactive filters and visualizations to allow users to dynamically explore the data and uncover trends that may not be immediately visible in raw datasets.

## 📍 State-Level Insights

The dashboard includes a detailed analysis of air quality patterns at the **state level**, helping users understand which regions experience higher pollution levels and which maintain relatively better air quality.

Several important patterns emerged during this analysis.

Some states recorded **AQI levels close to 500**, indicating extremely hazardous air quality conditions during certain time periods. These high values are typically associated with dense urban populations, industrial activity, heavy traffic, and unfavorable weather conditions that trap pollutants in the atmosphere.

States such as **Delhi, Himachal Pradesh, and Gujarat** show relatively higher average AQI values compared to many other states. Delhi in particular is widely recognized for experiencing severe air pollution episodes, especially during winter months when factors such as crop burning, temperature inversion, and reduced wind speed worsen air quality.

The dashboard allows users to view:

* **Highest AQI recorded in each state**
* **Lowest AQI recorded in each state**
* **Average AQI values for comparison**

By comparing the highest and lowest AQI values within each state, the dashboard reveals the **variability of air quality** across different locations within the same region.

This insight is particularly useful for environmental agencies and policymakers, as it helps identify **priority areas where pollution control measures may be most urgently needed**.

## 🌫 Pollutant Contribution Analysis

Air pollution is caused by multiple pollutants, each with different sources and environmental impacts.

The dashboard includes a **pollutant contribution analysis** that breaks down the relative share of major pollutants affecting air quality.

The analysis shows the following approximate contributions:

* **PM2.5 – ~30%**
* **PM10 – ~30%**
* **Ozone (O₃) – ~13%**
* **Carbon Monoxide (CO) – ~10%**
* **Nitrogen Dioxide (NO₂) – ~10%**
* **Sulfur Dioxide (SO₂) – smaller contribution**
* **Ammonia (NH₃) – smaller contribution**

These results clearly indicate that **particulate matter (PM2.5 and PM10)** is the **largest contributor to air pollution**.

Particulate matter consists of tiny particles suspended in the air that can enter the respiratory system and cause serious health problems.

Major sources of particulate matter include:

* Vehicle emissions
* Industrial processes
* Construction dust
* Burning of fossil fuels
* Agricultural residue burning

Because particulate matter contributes the largest share of pollution, many air quality improvement strategies focus on **reducing PM emissions**.
